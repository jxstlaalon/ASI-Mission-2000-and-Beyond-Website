PAST PROJECTS

Order of ASI Projects

2003 - St. Vincent

Mission 2003, was a foundational mission project in St. Vincent under the theme "Building on the Rock." The project ran for three weeks in July. A dedicated team of 85 missionaries served across 5 sites, focusing on health expos, door-to-door ministry, and Vacation Bible Schools. The project culminated with 32 individuals choosing to be baptized into God's kingdom. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2004 - Dominica

Mission 2004, was held in Dominica with the theme "Islands of Promise." Lasting through the month of August, 95 missionaries engaged in community service, including road cleaning and minor home repairs, which opened doors for Bible studies. The effort concluded with 28 souls accepting Christ through baptism. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2005 - St. Lucia

Mission 2005, took the gospel to St. Lucia under the banner "The Everlasting Gospel." For two weeks in August, 110 missionaries were mobilized across 7 parishes. Key activities included health seminars and nightly evangelistic meetings. The project saw a harvest of 41 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2006 - Antigua

Mission 2006, was a mission project in Antigua themed "Heaven's Call." Spanning the last two weeks of July and the first week of August, a team of 78 missionaries conducted children's programs and distributed literature. The faithful work resulted in 35 new believers being baptized. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2007 - London, England

Mission 2007, was a pioneering urban mission in London, England, under the theme "Light in the City." Throughout July, 125 missionaries engaged in street witnessing, soup kitchens, and public evangelism series in various communities. Despite the challenges of a secular environment, 22 precious souls were won for Christ. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2008 - Montego Bay, Jamaica

Mission 2008, was a vibrant project in Montego Bay, Jamaica, with the theme "Jubilee in Jamaica." Over three weeks in August, 150 missionaries served at 10 different sites. Activities included free medical clinics, concert ministries, and crusades. We rejoiced as 67 individuals gave their lives to Jesus in baptism. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2009 - Trinidad

Mission 2009, was held in Trinidad with the theme "Transformed by Truth." The project ran for the entire month of August and utilized 130 missionaries across 8 sites. A major community health fair attracted hundreds and created many Bible study interests. The mission concluded with 58 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2010 - Guyana

Mission 2010, returned to Guyana under the theme "Rooted in Love." A team of 120 missionaries served for two weeks in July, focusing on the interior regions. They conducted building projects for two schools and held evangelistic meetings. The project ended with 49 souls added to the church. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2011 - Tobago

Mission 2011, was a serene yet powerful project in Tobago, themed "Peace by the Sea." For two weeks in August, 80 missionaries focused on intimate community engagement, including fishing village visits and beachside worships. The project saw a beautiful harvest of 31 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2012 - Kingston, Jamaica

Mission 2012, was a dynamic mission in Kingston, Jamaica, under the theme "Kingston for the King." From late July to mid-August, 170 missionaries tackled 12 sites across the city. The project included urban gardening initiatives, youth empowerment seminars, and powerful crusades. A total of 89 individuals were baptized. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2013 - Costa Rica

Mission 2013, ventured to Costa Rica with the theme "Pura Vida en Cristo." Throughout August, 105 missionaries were divided into 6 teams, some working in city centers and others in rural villages. They conducted eye-glasses clinics and held meetings in both Spanish and English. The project yielded 54 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2014 - Belize

Mission 2014, was a mission project in Belize under the theme "A Firm Foundation." The project spanned the last two weeks of July. A team of 90 missionaries worked on constructing a church annex while simultaneously running health programs and children's ministries. This dual effort resulted in 44 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2015 - Dominican Republic

Mission 2015, was a large-scale project in the Dominican Republic themed "One Message, One Mission." Operating for three weeks in August, 200 missionaries flooded 15 sites. The project featured massive health expos, dental clinics, and nightly crusades that were broadcast locally. We celebrated a mighty harvest of 112 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2016 - Suriname

Mission 2016, was a cross-cultural mission in Suriname under the theme "A New Creation." For two weeks in August, 115 missionaries navigated multiple languages and cultures across 7 sites. Their work included riverboat medical missions and jungle evangelism. The project ended with 61 souls won for Christ. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2017 - Davíd, Panama

Mission 2017, was held in Davíd, Panama, with the theme "Faith in Action." Running through the month of July, 100 missionaries engaged in construction projects for a local orphanage and held evangelistic series in town squares. The project was blessed with 53 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2018 - Tobago

Mission 2018, was a return mission to Tobago under the theme "Abide in Me." This two-week project in August saw 88 missionaries focusing on deepening the work from previous years through small group discipleship and follow-up Bible studies. The faithful work resulted in 38 baptisms. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2019 - Guyana

Mission 2019, was a mission project in Guyana under the theme "No Longer Slaves," which spanned from the beginning of August. A total of 140 missionaries were gathered and divided into 9 different sites across the country. The project involved extensive community outreach, including street invitations, Bible studies, and diverse community projects such as painting schools, building a bridge, repairing a latrine, and filling potholes. This was a significant effort for the team, as over 60% of the missionaries were participating for the first time. At the end of Impact Guyana 2019, a total of 179 persons declared publicly by baptism that they were ransomed, redeemed, and no longer slaves. All Glory to God.

2022 - Curaçao

Mission 2022, marked a post-pandemic return to international missions in Curaçao under the theme "Reconnect & Restore." From August 6th to August 20th, a spirited team of 95 missionaries served across 8 sites. The project emphasized healing and community through mental health seminars, prayer walks, and vibrant crusades. The mission concluded with 48 souls reconnecting with Christ through baptism. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2023 - Medellín, Colombia

Mission 2023, was a transformative project in Medellín, Colombia, with the theme "City of Eternal Spring." Running from July 28th to August 19th, 128 missionaries were deployed to 10 sites across the city. The project featured innovative outreach through soccer ministries in local neighborhoods and cooking classes, alongside traditional crusades. By the end of the project, 87 individuals had decided to follow Jesus in baptism. All Glory to God. (False Information)

2024 - Barbados & Aguadulce, Panama

Mission 2024, had 2 mission projects: the first in Barbados and the second in Aguadulce, Panama. Impact Barbados was first, running from July 12th to July 27th. The project featured extensive pre-event outreach and utilized 112 participants across 14 different sites. At the end of the two weeks, 65 precious souls were added to the kingdom of God. Impact Panama began on July 31st and ended on August 24th. The project started with community service and health fairs across several districts, followed by two weeks of evangelism. This was a truly inter-generational effort, and we joyfully celebrated 2 weddings and 45 baptisms as the first fruits of their work. After both impacts, Impact Barbados and Impact Panama, a total of 110 souls were reached for Christ. All Glory to God.

2025 - Columbus, Ohio & Bogota, Colombia

Mission 2025, had 2 mission projects the first in Columbus, Ohio, USA and the second in Bogota, Colombia. Impact Ohio was first, that project spanned from July 19th to August 2nd. There was a total of 7 different sites, so 7 nightly crusades being run consecutively for 1 week then for the second week all 7 sites joined at Beacon of Hope SDA, Columbus Ohio and had the last week there as a grand crusade program. At the end of Impact Ohio there was 52 Baptisms while we were still there and the following week after we left there was a additional 11 baptisms from frequent crusade attenders who finally decided to give their life to Christ. Impact Columbia would have started on August 1st and would have ended on August 23rd. 96 missionaries were present in Impact Colombia and were divided to 11 different sites. 2 rehabilitation centers were also visited every morning and the occupants were ministered too. At the end of Impact Colombia, a total of 161 souls were reached for Christ. After Both impacts, Impact Ohio and Impact Colombia a total of 224 baptisms were had. All Glory to God.
